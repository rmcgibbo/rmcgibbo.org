I'm currently a second year PhD student in the Department of Chemistry at Stanford University. I work with Vijay Pande on understanding protein folding via long-timescale simulation and Markov state models.

I graduated from Princeton University, where I studied Chemistry and Computer Science. While there, I worked with Andrew Bocarsly.

In my free time, I read, follow politics, and rock climb. My GPG public key is here.

My research with Vijay Pande centers around building new techniques to extract physical insight from long timescale molecular dynamics simulations, typically for protein folding and conformational change.

My focus is on improving and accelerating the computational methods for molecular dynamics / Makov state model (MD / MSM) construction. Recently, this has involed applying machine learning techniques to the task of building structural distance metrics that explicitly capture kinetic aspects of protein dynamics. I also work on a framework distributed adaptive sampling MSM driven MD simulations.
