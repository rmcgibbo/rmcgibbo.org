I'm currently a third year PhD student in the Department of Chemistry at Stanford University. I work with Vijay Pande on quantitative statistical analysis of protein dynamics via long-timescale simulation and Markov models.

I graduated from Princeton University, where I studied Chemistry and Computer Science. While there, I worked with Andrew Bocarsly.

In my free time, I read, follow politics, and rock climb. My GPG public key is here.

My research with Vijay Pande centers around building new techniques for the quantitative statistical analysis of long timescale molecular dynamics simulations, typically for protein folding and conformational change.

This work has involved both theory, software development, and applications. Aspects have involved distance metric learning, using large-margin techniques, the development of novel L1-regularized hidden Markov models for analysis of protein dynamics (including a massively accelerated multicore and CUDA implementations), and variational methods for model selection.

Most of this research requires writing new software, and scalability is a central priority for our work on massive protein dynamics datasets. This work is all publicly available on github.
