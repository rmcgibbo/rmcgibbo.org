Robert T. McGibbon

I am currently a research scientist at D.E. Shaw Research, where I work on the development of more accurate physics-based models for molecular dynamics simulations.

Previously, I did my Ph.D. in the Department of Chemistry at Stanford University, where I worked with Vijay Pande at the intersection of biophysics and machine learning on quantitative statistical analysis of protein dynamics via long-timescale molecular simulation and the development of parsimonious statistical models of these statistical data. My Ph.D. thesis is available here. Beforehand, I was an undergraduate from Princeton University, where I studied Chemistry and Computer Science. While there, I worked with Andrew Bocarsly.

Research

My research at Stanford with Vijay Pande centered around building new techniques for the quantitative statistical analysis of long timescale molecular dynamics simulations, typically for protein folding and conformational change.

This work has involved both theory, software development, and applications. Aspects have involved distance metric learning, using large-margin techniques, the development of novel L1-regularized hidden Markov models for analysis of protein dynamics (including a massively accelerated multicore and CUDA implementations), and variational methods for model selection.

Most of this research requires writing new software, and scalability is a central priority for our work on massive protein dynamics datasets. This work is all publicly available on github.
